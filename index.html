Bio

I am a graduate student in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Vanderbilt University and work as a research assistant at the Institute for Software Integrated Systems with my advisor Abhishek Dubey, PhD. My research aims at making cities more assessible and efficient through intelligent software systems. This includes looking for ways to integrate sensing technologies with the physical world to improve services in urban environments. My expertise lies in designing algorithms for real-time decision-making in large-scale systems through data-driven methods as well as decentralized compute architectures for societal-scale cyber-physical systems. I have experience working with a variety of open-source machine learning, data science, cloud-based processing and data management technologies.

I worked as a Research Intern at Dataminr in the summer of 2022 where I worked on alerting and detection using representation learning, graph neural networks and natural language processing. I received my M.S. degree in Civil Engineering from Northwestern University in December 2018 and my Bachelor degree in Civil Engineering from The University of Notre Dame in 2012. Additionally, I worked a structural engineer for five years before starting my PhD.
